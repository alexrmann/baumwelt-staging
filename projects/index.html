Projects

Our firm brings extensive experience in the planning and design of parks, open spaces, and community-focused recreation areas. Our project strengths include sustainable design, restoration ecology, sportsfields, campuses, and accessible sites. We’ve partnered with a broad range of municipal and public agencies delivering projects that are community-driven, code-compliant, and cost-conscious.

Featured Projects

A description to provide context to why these are important, and thus featured.

  • Volunteer Field Infield Conversion

    Baumwelt led the design for the conversion of the baseball infield at Volunteer Field in Port Angeles from a traditional dirt surface to a high-performance synthetic turf infield, improving playability and drainage while supporting a community-driven construction effort.

  • Givens Sport Courts Improvements

    Renovation of sports courts at Givens Park in Port Orchard, transforming aging facilities into flexible, multi-sport courts that support tennis, pickleball, basketball, and futsal while improving safety, accessibility, and neighborhood connections.

  • Woodbine Columbarium Addition

    Baumwelt designed the expansion of the columbarium garden at Woodbine Cemetery for the City of Puyallup, developing a thoughtful layout that increased future capacity while working carefully around existing structures and honoring the dignity of the site.

  • Tribal Wellness Campus

    Baumwelt prepared an updated site plan for the Lower Elwha Klallam Tribe’s proposed Wellness Campus near Port Angeles, modernizing an earlier master plan and organizing a cohesive campus layout that supports health services, community gathering, and long-term growth.

  • Arletta Schoolhouse Landscape Renovation

    Baumwelt led the landscape and site design for the renovation of the historic Arletta Schoolhouse in Gig Harbor for PenMet Parks, supporting the adaptive reuse of the building while enhancing the surrounding grounds for community gatherings and public events.

  • Tacoma DeMolay Sandspit Park Improvements

    Baumwelt led the design for major accessibility and facility improvements at Tacoma DeMolay Sandspit Park for PenMet Parks, creating ADA-compliant access from the uplands to the shoreline while enhancing visitor amenities and supporting marine recreation along the Tacoma waterfront.

  • Neighborhood Greens Minigolf Renovation

    Baumwelt led the design and renovation of the Neighborhood Greens Mini Golf course for PenMet Parks, transforming a deteriorated and abandoned facility into a vibrant, Pacific Northwest–themed attraction that now serves as both a community destination and a successful revenue-generating amenity for the park district.

  • Sehmel Homestead Park Multiuse Field Conversion

    Baumwelt led the design and construction administration for the replacement of the synthetic turf multiuse field at Sehmel Homestead Park for PenMet Parks, resolving long-standing drainage issues while upgrading the facility to better support community sports and events.

  • Lower Wapato Creek Habitat Project

    The Lower Wapato Creek Habitat Project restored approximately 20 acres of tidally influenced estuary and fish-bearing stream habitat for the Port of Tacoma through major hydrologic and ecological restoration, including stream realignment, wetland creation, and barrier removal to improve fish passage and habitat quality.

  • Logger Baseball Field Infield Conversion

    Transformation of traditional dirt infield and natural grass outfield into a high-performance synthetic turf facility designed to meet NCAA standards.

  • Logger Softball Field Improvements

    Baumwelt is leading the design and construction administration for major improvements to the University of Puget Sound’s Logger Softball Facility, transforming an aging and difficult-to-view field into a modern collegiate venue with improved player amenities, spectator experience, and field performance.
