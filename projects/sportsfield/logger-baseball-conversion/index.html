Logger Baseball Field Conversion

Transformation of traditional dirt infield and natural grass outfield into a high-performance synthetic turf facility designed to meet NCAA standards.

As the sportsfield designer and project manager, Derrick and his firm are leading the transformation of the University of Puget Sound’s Logger Baseball Field into a high-performance synthetic turf facility designed to meet NCAA standards. This project converts the traditional dirt infield and natural grass outfield into a state-of-the-art synthetic turf infield, addressing both performance and player safety needs for collegiate baseball.

An innovative subsurface drainage system was incorporated into the design, featuring a shock pad with integrated drainage grooves. This approach reduced the drainage footprint—resulting in cost savings while simultaneously enhancing player safety by mitigating the risk of head injuries and joint stress. This dual-benefit design solution exemplifies the firm’s commitment to both function and athlete well-being.

Derrick worked closely with the university to guide them through key decisions, providing resources such as site visits to previous installations and access to past clients. This collaborative process ensured the university felt fully informed and confident in their selections. The design also incorporates updates aligned with recent NCAA rule changes, ensuring the field will be compliant for upcoming seasons.
